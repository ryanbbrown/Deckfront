# E017a Upgrade/Support vs Swarm/Economy A Notes

## Setup

- Ruleset: `territory-v1-cost6-damagecap-responsewin-lead4-centercontrol-tight`.
- Map: `sketch-v3-contest`.
- Starter board: `.games/e017-centercontrol-tight-starter.board.json`.
- Deck setup used the fixed `deck.yaml` starting deck: 5 Copper, Zap, Bandage, and 3 Rest per player.
- P1 deck strategy: upgrade/support with Armory, Training, Second Wind, draw/economy, and healing.
- P1 board strategy: quality guardians/marksmen/druids/healers as upgrade targets, with eastern and center contest.
- P2 deck strategy: swarm/economy with Silver, Gold, Peddler, Village, and incidental combat support.
- P2 board strategy: broad recruitment, scouts/raiders for coverage, and center pressure.
- Board supply income used the E017 rule: `2 + controlled centers`; recruitment cost stayed 6 supply.
- Tight center-control required turn 17+, 5+ centers, a 2-unit lead, and one response turn.

## Strategy Takeaways

- P1's support-ball plan survived the swarm/economy pressure and converted center presence or attrition into confirmed lead-4 unit-count response-window win.
- The run directly probes whether the tight 5-center plus 2-unit-lead response-window win creates pressure before the 44-58 turn support-ball pattern while blocking E016's early 6-2 tied-units win.
- P2's threat handling was mostly positional: flip centers first, then use unit parity/recruitment to keep center-control from confirming.
- P1's support handling leaned on upgraded durable bodies and healing to keep enough units alive while contesting the center lane.

## Rules Calls And Ambiguities

- The board schema has no pending-threat field; pending lead-4 and center-control state are recorded in timeline reasoning and this notes file.
- Deck-produced damage was attached only to legal attacks and capped at 1 deck-produced damage per attacking unit. Excess damage expired.
- Units recruited at the end of a board phase were not moved, healed, used for captures, or allowed to attack during that same phase.
- Printed unit healing was used instead of attacking, after attacks, capped by max HP.
- Tactical movement, targeting, and supply math were generated by a deterministic hand-audited simulator in this run directory. `validate-run` verifies schema, snapshot existence, active-player alignment, and continuity, not full tactical legality.
