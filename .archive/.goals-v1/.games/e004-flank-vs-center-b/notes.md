# E004 Flank vs Center B Notes

## Strategy Assignment

- P1 deck: healing/control with opportunistic damage. P1 prioritized Potion/Healer/Village/Peddler/Storm lines and never bought Copper.
- P1 board: from the northeast starting side, P1 interpreted the assigned south/southeast flank as pressure through center-east and center-southeast before bending into center-center.
- P1 units: scouts took the flank captures, raiders punished isolated center units, and druid/healer support used printed healing when no attack was better.
- P2 deck: balanced economy into upgrades and tactical damage. P2 bought Training/Blast/Peddler/Silver style cards and never bought Copper.
- P2 board: from the southwest starting side, P2 contested central supply with a compact guardian/marksman/druid formation and respected delayed recruits.
- Expected tension: first-player/side control, testing whether P2 center pressure can overcome P1's seat-side flank tempo.

## Rules Calls And Ambiguities

- Fixed starting decks were used exactly as assigned instead of the locked variant's default 7 Copper plus draft budget. The ruleset says this should be recorded and evidence quality downgraded.
- Deck damage was always attached to a legal unit attack. No damage was treated as global direct damage.
- Storm-style spread damage never became relevant.
- Recruited units were not moved, attacked, healed, used for reattack, or used to capture on the turn they entered.
- Printed healing was used instead of attacking for the listed druid/healer actions.
- Movement used odd-column offsets from the locked board rules. The helper allowed paths through friendly occupied hexes because the locked movement rule only forbids paths through occupied enemy hexes and forbids ending on any occupied hex.
