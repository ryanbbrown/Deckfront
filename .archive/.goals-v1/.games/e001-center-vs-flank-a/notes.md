# E001 Center vs Flank A Notes

## Strategy Assignment

- P1 deck: balanced economy into tactical damage; avoided Copper buys.
- P1 board: contested central supply centers with guardian/marksman formation and scout support.
- P2 deck: healing/control plus opportunistic attacks; avoided Copper buys.
- P2 board: flanked through south/southeast, forcing P1 to split from center.

## Rules Ambiguities And Calls

- The required starter board snapshot has damaged units even though the rules say units start at max HP. I preserved the required snapshot and treated newly recruited units as entering at max HP.
- Unit heal values are listed for druid/healer, but board-rules do not specify heal timing/range. I used a conservative unit action interpretation: druid heals 1 adjacent/self, healer heals 1 within range 2, once per board phase instead of attacking.
- Deck damage was treated as extra damage assigned only alongside otherwise legal attacks, not as free direct damage.
- Supply center control persisted after units left or died, changing only when an enemy unit ended movement on that center.
- The unit-lead win condition was treated as giving the opponent one full response turn after a lead is created; no win was called on the opponent response turn solely because the inactive player had the lead.

## Stopping Point

Stopped after 16 completed player turns. P1 led 8 units to 7 and held four centers; P2 held three centers, had active support pieces, and continued to threaten the flank. No start-of-turn unit-lead win condition had resolved.
